Tianlong Zhong

PhD Candidate in Education | Nanyang Technological University

I am a Ph.D. candidate whose research lies at the intersection of artificial intelligence in education, multimodal learning analytics, and human-AI interaction in programming learning. My work uses multimodal data to examine learners’ affective, behavioral, and cognitive processes, with a particular focus on how these processes dynamically interact during AI-assisted learning. A distinctive contribution of my research is that it goes beyond measuring learning outcomes to uncover the mechanisms through which students think, feel, act, and maintain agency when learning with AI. More importantly, my work aims to advance learning theory in the age of AI, particularly by re-examining information processing theory through multimodal empirical evidence.

About

My work examines how learners engage with emerging technologies, including generative AI, programming environments, multimodal learning analytics, and computational thinking activities. I am particularly interested in how human-AI interaction can support learners' thinking processes, problem solving, and interdisciplinary learning.
